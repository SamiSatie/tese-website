Research

Concepts, methods and tools for conversion chains; control, digital twins and energy systems.

Scientific areas

Two pillars, four clear axes.

The 2026 TeSE presentation structures the team around conversion chains and the control of energy systems.

Axis 1

New transducer concepts

Flux-switching machines, hybrid-excited machines, variable-flux memory machines, multiphase and fractional architectures.

Axis 2

Modelling and optimisation

Low- and high-frequency modelling, iron-loss prediction, differential/common-mode impedances, topology and parametric optimisation.

Axis 3

Control and digital twins

Advanced control, real-time simulation, digital implementation, health monitoring and fault diagnosis.

Axis 4

Networks and multi-vector systems

Control and energy management of microgrids, AC/DC networks, multi-source systems and battery/fuel-cell hybridisation.

Cross-cutting

Resilience and eco-design

Trade-offs between efficiency, mass, environmental cost, repairability, diagnosability and degraded-mode operation.

Experimental

Measurement and validation

Laser vibrometry, machine benches, precise performance/loss measurements and model-prototype updating.

Central research challenge

How can we design electromechanical conversion chains that are more efficient, compact, fault-resilient and environmentally responsible from the earliest design stages?
