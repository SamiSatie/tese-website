Positioning

A team at the core of sustainable electrification.

TeSE operates at the interface of electrical machines, power electronics, real-time control, digital twins and experimental validation.

Mission

Design

New transducers, unconventional machines, fractional architectures, low-voltage conversion chains and multi-source systems.

Methods

Model and optimise

Finite-element, analytical and semi-analytical models; parametric, topology, multiphysics and robust optimisation.

Validation

Control and test

Machine benches, loss measurements, vibrometry, resilient control, HIL, FPGA and embedded digital twins.

Applications

Low-carbon mobility, embedded systems and energy networks.

SATIE’s 2025-2030 roadmap highlights safe, efficient and sustainable mobility, resilient electrical systems and the energy transition.

Electric mobilityAdvanced air mobilityHigh-speed machinesAC/DC smart gridsHydrogen and multi-source systemsEco-designDiagnosis and resilience
Evidence

Demonstrators and software supporting technology transfer.

The team highlights the CTAF project, electrical machine test benches, the 5 kW technological showcase and the e-Pivot software developed with Stellantis since 2017.
