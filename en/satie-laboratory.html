SATIE — the laboratory behind the TeSE team

TeSE is a research team of SATIE, a joint research unit dedicated to systems and applications of information and energy technologies.

Short overview

A major research laboratory at the interface of information, energy and engineering systems.

SATIE — Systems and Applications of Information and Energy Technologies — conducts engineering-science research on electrical energy systems, information systems, measurement, signal processing, instrumentation and technologies supporting energy, industrial and societal transitions.

Within this framework, TeSE focuses on transducers, electrical machines, electromechanical conversion chains, advanced control, digital twins and the associated experimental platforms.

Key points

SATIE in a few words.

This page is a short summary. It does not replace the official information available on SATIE’s institutional website.

Research

The laboratory covers electrical engineering, energy systems, information systems, instrumentation, imaging, signal processing and complex systems.

Platforms

SATIE operates experimental resources for characterisation, measurement, system validation and energy platforms.

Education and transfer

The laboratory contributes to research-based education, doctoral supervision, academic and industrial partnerships and technology transfer.

Institutions

A multi-institution academic environment.

SATIE brings together an ecosystem involving CNRS, ENS Paris-Saclay, CNAM, CY Cergy Paris Université, Université Gustave Eiffel and Université Paris-Saclay.
